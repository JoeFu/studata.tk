Project Introduction

Project Name: Organised Access to Historical Student Data

Supervisors: Nick Falkner, Claudia Szabo, Katrina Falkner

Availability: "COMP SCI 2008 Topics in Computer Science","COMP SCI 3020 Adv Topics in Computer Science","COMP SCI 4015 Honours Computer Science Research Project","COMP SCI 7096 Master of Software Engineering Project","COMP SCI 7098 Master of Computing & Innovation Project"

Contact: nickolas.falkner@adelaide.edu.au

Project Description

Introduction The school of Computer Science regularly analyses data collected as part of teaching to improve teaching practice and to identify better ways to assess and evaluate student work. Over the years, we have accumulated a large set of data and the problem now is organising it for searching and trend analysis.

The project You will design and build a database for storing, retrieving and organising at least two of the school's pedagogical data sources, providing a GUI-front end to allow a member of staff to query the database, extract data, aggregate it, and export it. You will also be required to write extraction and insertion scripts that will work with the file-based storage for the current data.

Important: All data will be anonymised and will be identified in a way that allows us to associate actions without identifying the students.

Requirements You do not need to understand statistics or database queries in detail. This aspect of the project will be guided by the contacts but you will be expected to read and implement certain algorithms as part of the project.

Multiple offerings This project may be offered in multiple ways if students of different levels are interested. Advanced Topics and Honours students should expect to add anonymisation or privacy preserving aspects to their project. Don't stress, we'll take you through what that means.

Related Work

There are currently some online learning platforms or discussion forums from other schools and universities which mainly provide an online-school environment for students to get access to the online resources. Our team has been primarily focusing on the students' online activities and behaviours. Our online system can both record and analyze the students' online behaviours, such as viewing, creating, and enrollment. This system can also help visualize data through visual graphs based on our customers' requirement. The current platform, which is supported by Restful APIs, can be viewed by many web-browsers in multiple devices. Fundamentally, the system can support to import data from two data sources in the current stage: the web submission system (including old and new data systems in file directory structure) and the Moodle forum system.
